Early Life

Eminem was born in St. Joseph, Missouri, and raised in Detroit, Michigan. He had a troubled childhood, growing up in poverty and facing family issues.

He discovered his passion for rapping and writing at a young age, using it as an escape from his difficult circumstances.

Career

Eminem's rap career began in the late 1990s when he adopted the stage name "M&M," which he later altered to "Eminem." He gained attention through underground rap battles and released his debut album "Infinite" in 1996.

However, it was his second major-label album, "The Slim Shady LP" (1999), that brought him widespread recognition and success. The album won him his first Grammy Award for Best Rap Album.

Subsequent albums like "The Marshall Mathers LP" (2000) and "The Eminem Show" (2002) further solidified his place as one of the greatest rappers of all time.

Eminem's music often explores personal struggles, his relationships, and the challenges he faced in his career, making him a relatable and influential artist.

Acting Career

Eminem has also explored acting and made his debut in the semi-autobiographical film "8 Mile" (2002), where he played the lead character, Jimmy Smith Jr., a struggling young rapper. The film was a critical and commercial success, and Eminem's performance earned praise, including an Academy Award for Best Original Song for "Lose Yourself."
